## Research Focus

# From Process-Driven Acceleration to Agentic, Spec-Driven Engineering

A practical framework to reallocate engineering effort from manual coding to AI agents orchestrated by high-quality specifications — increasing speed, predictability, and architectural integrity.

---

### STEP 01

## Design High-Quality Specifications

Acceleration begins with specification maturity. Instead of concentrating effort on manual coding, the focus shifts to precise domain modeling, explicit architectural boundaries, structured non-functional requirements, and deterministic acceptance criteria. When 70–80% of effort is invested in specification design, AI agents can operate with clarity, consistency, and reduced ambiguity.

---

### STEP 02

## Orchestrate Agentic Execution

With well-defined specifications, AI agents become structured execution engines. They generate architecture scaffolds, consistent implementations, automated tests, and documentation with minimal iterative prompting. Human involvement shifts from coding to validation, architectural oversight, and strategic refinement — restoring rigor to AI-assisted development.

---

### STEP 03

## Validate, Govern, and Evolve

Agentic systems require structured oversight. Automated validation pipelines ensure generated artifacts remain aligned with specifications, architectural constraints, and non-functional requirements. Humans act as architectural guardians, reviewing outputs at defined checkpoints while allowing agents to iterate within controlled boundaries. This governance layer preserves quality, prevents drift, and enables safe, continuous evolution of the system.

---

## Expected Result: Enable Rapid Experimentation at Scale

As specification maturity increases, manual coding effort decreases. Organizations gain the ability to prototype multiple strategies, test business variations, and target different customer segments in parallel. Agentic development preserves architectural integrity while dramatically reducing time-to-experiment and delivery risk.

And the benefit becomes implicit:
Controlled autonomy → Faster experimentation → Reduced delivery risk
